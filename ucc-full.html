UCC Challenges Learning Institutions on Problem-Solving Innovations

August 1, 2025 — Kampala | By Ocungirwoth Jordan

The Executive Director of the Uganda Communications Commission (UCC), Mr. Nyombi Tembo, urged learning institutions to focus on innovations that address national challenges such as climate change, youth unemployment, and digital inclusion.

Speaking at the 9th National Conference on Communications held at the UCC offices in Kampala, Mr. Tembo emphasized the role of universities in leading technological change through research-based problem solving.

“We have brilliant students who just need the right support and platforms. This country’s problems won’t be solved from abroad; we must empower our own institutions to lead the way,” Mr. Tembo stated.

The conference attracted stakeholders from academia, the private sector, and government, including Mr. Geoffrey Tabo Olale, the head of Computer Science at Gulu University.

Mr. Nyombi made the remarks at the launch of the annual 9th National Conference on Communications 2025 organised by the UCC in partnership with Gulu and ISBAT University.
