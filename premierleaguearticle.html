Premier League Kicks Off On the 15th of August

Posted: August 3, 2025 | 2:15 PM | Tags: #Football #EPL

Manchester United will host Arsenal at Old Trafford in a blockbuster fixture on the opening weekend of the 2025/26 Premier League. Champions Liverpool begin their title defense at home to AFC Bournemouth.

See who every Premier League club will face in Matchweek 1 of the 2025/26 season. United vs Arsenal is the standout, while Liverpool’s defense begins under the Friday night lights.

Manchester United hosts Arsenal in a high-profile clash. Meanwhile, Liverpool kicks off against Bournemouth, a team they’ve consistently dominated at Anfield.

Check Out All Premier League Fixtures

Arsenal will look to end their 22-year title drought. Despite their poor away record at Old Trafford, they’ll aim for a strong start.

Arsenal will face a Manchester United side that finished 15th last season, though new manager Ruben Amorim has signed Matheus Cunha from Wolves to bolster their attack.

Gunners boss Mikel Arteta expects a busy transfer window. The start of their season is tough, facing several top-seven teams early on.

Liverpool Start Title Defense at Anfield

Liverpool’s opener is against Bournemouth. They’ve won seven of their last eight at Anfield against the Cherries, including a 9-0 win in 2022.

Arne Slot's men have a tough run: Newcastle away, Arsenal at home, Everton in the Merseyside derby, and Chelsea away — all within the first two months.

Burnley Test for Frank's Spurs Debut

Thomas Frank’s Spurs debut will be against Burnley, whose defense was stellar last season. It's a huge test for the new coach.

Sunderland hosts West Ham, and Leeds faces Everton under the lights at Elland Road. Everton’s home debut at their new stadium will be in Matchweek 2.

Man City Start at Revitalised Wolves

Manchester City’s first test is away at Wolves, who were transformed under Vitor Pereira. Elsewhere, three top-11 battles spice up the weekend.

  • Friday 15 August
    20:00 – Liverpool v AFC Bournemouth
  • Saturday 16 August
    12:30 – Aston Villa v Newcastle
    15:00 – Brighton v Fulham
    15:00 – Nottingham Forest v Brentford
    15:00 – Sunderland v West Ham
    15:00 – Tottenham v Burnley
    17:30 – Wolves v Manchester City
  • Sunday 17 August
    14:00 – Chelsea v Crystal Palace
    16:30 – Manchester United v Arsenal
  • Monday 18 August
    20:00 – Leeds v Everton

The 2025/26 season concludes on Sunday 24 May 2026 with all final fixtures kicking off simultaneously.
